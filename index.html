I help companies figure out what to build, why it matters, and how to talk about it.

I grew up on a farm in Turkey. I was the first in my family to go to university. I studied computer science, then got into one of Europe's most competitive design programmes at Aalto, with no design background. My graduation project was a game where the player never needed to look at a screen. The physical world was the interface. That instinct, to make technology fit around people rather than the other way around, is still what drives my work.

I co-founded an energy risk management startup. I went from knowing nobody in the sector to sitting across from energy executives and securing pilots. I've moved countries four times. I'm either very adaptable or very restless. Probably both.

I have a habit of telling people what's broken in their business. An academic became a consultant. An astrologer doubled her clients by cutting her session time in half. If your product or business has something broken in it, I'll probably notice. We should talk.

How to Work With Me

I diagnose before I prescribe. I go quiet early, then come back with something grounded.

I ask relentlessly about context, constraints, history, and the stuff nobody wrote down. That's how I find the real problem.

I think in systems and visuals, and I work best when given a real problem and room to move.

I'll challenge assumptions (including yours, and especially mine) because that's where the good work happens.

Tell me the outcome you need. I'll figure out the path.
